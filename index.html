An old adage states "You catch a man a fish and he eats for a day; you teach a man to fish and he eats for life". I don't really know what this means, but I do know that professor Pixton proved the Riemann Hypothesis in class.

Rieman Hypothesis

As a homework problem, Prof Pixton asked us to prove or disprove PNP. When I complained that it was too hard because it was a Millenium Prize Problem, he proved the Riemann Hypothesis in front of the whole class to show that Millenium Prize Problems are no harder than textbook problems. Truly brilliant guy, I'll never look at math in the same way.
